History of Gaming

1972: The Magnavox Odyssey

The Magnavox Odyssey—the world’s first home gaming console—hit living rooms in 1972, selling 300,000 units.

1977: Atari 2600

The Atari 2600 brings variety to home gaming. Fan favorites like Space Invaders help push sales to 30 million units.

1983: Nintendo Famicom

Japan is introduced to the Family Computer. It’s renamed the Nintendo Entertainment System (NES) for release in the US in 1985.

1985: The Revival with Nintendo

The release of the Nintendo Entertainment System (NES) in 1985 revitalized the gaming industry. With iconic games like Super Mario Bros., Nintendo laid the foundation for modern console gaming.

1989: Game Boy

Mobile gaming goes mainstream with the release of the Nintendo Game Boy, selling 118 million units of the 8-bit handheld system.

1994: Sony PlayStation

Engineered as a disc-based system, the Sony PlayStation rapidly gains momentum after its release in 1994, selling 125 million units worldwide.

1995: The 3D Revolution

The mid-90s saw the rise of 3D gaming with the release of consoles like the Sony PlayStation and the Nintendo 64. Games like Final Fantasy VII and Super Mario 64 revolutionized gaming with 3D graphics.

2001: The Rise of Online Multiplayer

The release of the Xbox in 2001 and the introduction of Xbox Live brought online multiplayer gaming to the forefront. Halo 2 became one of the first major online multiplayer games.

2003: Second Life

Second Life revolutionizes 3D environments and virtual worlds. By 2013, in-game spending in virtual goods reaches $3.2 billion.

2004: World of Warcraft

World of Warcraft opens its virtual fantasy doors. At launch, the popular MMORPG has over 10 million subscribers. By 2012, it generates $10 billion in revenue.

2007: Mobile Gaming Takes Off

With the launch of the iPhone in 2007, mobile gaming exploded. Games like Angry Birds and Clash of Clans reached millions of players, making mobile gaming a massive industry.

2010: Kinect for Xbox 360

In response to Nintendo Wii’s motion-controlled games, Microsoft releases Kinect for Xbox 360, allowing players to use their bodies as motion controllers.

2011: Nintendo 3DS

At just 8.5 ounces, Nintendo’s 3DS is the first portable game system with masterful glasses-free 3D display.

2020: The Age of Streaming and Cloud Gaming

The rise of streaming platforms like Twitch, YouTube Gaming, and cloud gaming services such as Google Stadia and Xbox Cloud Gaming changed the way players engage with games.
